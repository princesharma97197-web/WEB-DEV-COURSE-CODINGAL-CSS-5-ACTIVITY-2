Birds are warm-blooded, feathered vertebrates characterized by wings, beaks, laying hard-shelled eggs, and a high metabolism, existing in over 11,000 species globally, from tiny hummingbirds to large ostriches, with adaptations for diverse environments like flight, swimming, or running, playing vital ecological roles in seed dispersal, pest control, and pollination.
